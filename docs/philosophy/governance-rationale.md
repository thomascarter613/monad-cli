# Governance Rationale

This document explains why governance is a first-class part of Monad.

## Why Governance Matters

Software repositories fail slowly when decisions, constraints, risks, and evidence drift away from implementation.

Monad treats governance as part of the repository operating model because serious systems need to explain:

- why artifacts exist;
- which decisions shaped them;
- which policies apply;
- what risks are known;
- what evidence proves readiness;
- what changed and why.

## Governance Is Not Bureaucracy

Governance should make change safer and clearer, not slower for its own sake.

Good governance is:

- explicit;
- local-first;
- testable;
- automatable;
- reviewable;
- proportionate to risk;
- honest about uncertainty.

## Why ADRs

ADRs preserve durable decisions so future contributors do not rediscover or reverse important tradeoffs accidentally.

## Why Work Packets

Work packets connect roadmap intent to implementation scope, tasks, acceptance criteria, and evidence.

## Why Traceability

Traceability lets Monad answer how requirements, decisions, work, tests, risks, and releases connect.

## Why Policy-as-Code

Policies turn governance expectations into repeatable checks. They reduce reliance on memory and manual review.

## Maintenance Notes

Governance should remain useful and lightweight enough to support implementation, not obstruct it.
